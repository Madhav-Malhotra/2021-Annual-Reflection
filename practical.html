During the last 1.5 years of COVID, I spent 85% of every day alone in my house.

This harmed my mental health and relationships a lot. I lost the best friend I’ve ever had in 2021.

I want to share the lessons I learned from this year’s struggle. I hope they help you too :-)

Mental Health

Everyone’s fighting two battles: them vs. themselves and them vs. the world.

The battle with myself is more important. If I don’t care about myself, I won’t grow. I won’t even want to live.

Self-esteem is the prerequisite to ANY success.

When struggling, be the supporter I wish I had. Plan what’d make me happy. Do that for others.

Others may not be there for me. That’s out of my control. When that happens, I can help others. That’s in my control.

I’ll stop thinking of just me, me, me. And I’ll feel compassion. Evolutionary guarantee!

Don’t make decisions while I’m feeling negative. Wait a day before communicating with others.

Don’t be a victim. Self-pity makes me unhappy. It doesn’t solve problems.

Notice when I have thoughts like ‘this is so unfair.’ Respond: “Don’t be a victim. What’s the first step to fixing this?”

Depression, anxiety, loneliness, etc. are feelings. If a solution makes me feel better, it works.

It doesn’t matter if the solution seems weird.

  • Try pointing at myself and laughing for no reason.
  • Try imagining that an alien is in front of me. It has the EXACT same problems as me. What advice would I give it?
  • Try making a deal with landmarks like trees: “I bet so many humans that pass you aren’t smiling. So I’ll make you a deal, tree. I’ll start smiling and you can have better sights to see! Win win!”

Get over the self-doubt. Then I can actually be useful to others.

Don’t push away negative thoughts. Don’t ruminate over them endlessly. Seek the balanced middle. 

Ex: Letting myself recount a sad experience by journaling about it. But closing the journal after and not thinking about it.

Mild physical discomfort clears away mental discomfort. Ex: Cold air on my skin.

Venting emotions to someone is a healthy habit. If I’m not doing that, I’m doing something wrong.

Productivity

Schedule time to do nothing. Just stop and think. I’ll find new perspectives on old problems.

This works on all sorts of problems. From relationship issues to debugging code.

Action needs intention. Action doesn’t need tangibility.

Sometimes, it takes all the courage I can muster to do nothing. Ex: Not check notifications :D

Other times, it takes all the courage I can muster to do something tiny. Ex: break a promise I can’t honor anymore.

If I don’t have a goal, I literally cannot win.

If I don’t have a goal, I can’t compare different ways of reaching it. (To find the best way)

Before starting any work, write down WHY I’m working on it.

Write down the minority of the work that has the majority of the value.

And break down the work into smaller steps to do.

When stuck on something for a long time, work backwards

Ask: "What skills will I need to reach my goal? Which experts can I ask to find out?”

Work with a countdown timer running. It forces me to cut through what doesn’t matter.

Ex: 10 minutes to finish these emails, 30 minutes to finish this article, so on.

Work in sprints. Ex: 15-minute bursts. At the end of each sprint, ask: “Am I still working on the real problem? Have I gotten off track?”

“You don't need to push yourself as hard when curiosity is pulling you.” - Paul Graham

Schedule 15-20 minutes in a day to just research something random out of curiosity. 

Structure is like a dam that slows down a river of creativity.

If I can’t get 10 good ideas, get 20 ideas.

Intentionally come up with stupid ideas even! Combine stupid ideas into good ideas to get very creative ideas no one’s thought of.

I can create or I can polish. I can’t do both.

The first time doing something creative, make it rough! If it feels ‘scalable’, I did it wrong.

If demotivated, work on most exciting part of my discipline. That’ll motivate me to work on the regular parts after.

Ex: AI doesn’t have many tangible outcomes. So I worked on a project making filtered profile pictures with AI. I gifted these to friends. This tangibility motivated me.

There’s 10% better and 10x better.

To get to 10x better, ask: “What can X do that can’t be done anywhere else?” 

^ For any topic X or even any person X.

The areas I haven’t prioritised are the areas I can get 10x better with just a tiny effort.

Ex: I know nothing about sewing. I could probably learn how to apply a patch to clothing in 10 minutes.

I can save more time not working unnecessarily than by not working inefficiently.

At the beginning of the day, ask: “What’s the least necessary thing I was planning to work on today?” Don’t do that or do that last.

Relationships

Supporting others isn’t simple. But there are some key pieces:

  1. Show I care. Tell others my intention is to help.
  2. Let them talk as much as possible. Ask open-ended questions. Leave silence to think. Ask for examples. Try saying back what they said.
  3. Ask them what they need. Don’t tell them what they should do.

I don’t need to be in control to feel calm. If I accept what’s out of my control, I’ll also feel calm.

This often works with relationship issues: “They’re going to [verb] and it’s out of my control. Oh well.”

Not everything happens with intention and not everything with intention happens.

Ex: Fun and relationships both seem to grow in spontaneity. Go into a meeting without plans once a month.

I don’t make friends by having others show interest/appreciation for me. I make friends by showing interest/appreciation for others.

Go on social media and post compliments on the first 10 posts I see. Even if they’re ads.

Defer conflicts until I’m face-to-face with the other. It’s hard to understand the meaning behind words over text.

Look beyond results. Ask others about their intentions. Especially in conflict/failure.

At the start of a day, remind myself of the things that may go wrong. And realise those possibilities aren’t as bad as they seem. 

It’s not about expecting bad things to happen. But acknowledge bad things may happen.

This works well when planning tasks for the day (ex: X may not show up to the call).

I, others, and the world are always changing.

If I feel upset at ______, say: “This too shall pass.”

Nothing matters as much as I think in the long run. To realise this, go on a walk for 5 minutes and ask: “WHY am I [verb]?”

Death is weird. Being reminded of it creates messy emotions. But also some benefits:

  • Remind myself that I’ll do [current action] for the last time one day. Maybe even today.
  • Remind myself I might look back on some part of this current moment wistfully. Because I’ll likely have more problems as I get older and closer to death.

Both will make me appreciate what I’m doing.

Self-awareness

When I feel afraid, dig deeper. There’s an important cause there that I don’t yet understand.

Ask: “Which of my prior experiences might have influenced this action/decision?”

Only I can decide what makes life worth living.

A helpful piece of information in this decision is my values. Ask: “What tiny things easily make me feel irritated?” Those are my non-negotiable values being broken.

"What past limitations - real or perceived - are you still carrying on as baggage?" - Tim Ferris

It’s easy to let what’s expected mask what’s possible.

I have several instincts which tend to give the wrong result consistently. Ex: Being hesitant to try new things. Start a decisions tracker - any time I have to think twice about a decision:

  1. Write the problem
  2. Write what I considered
  3. Write the decision I made
  4. Later, write its outcome.

Over time, I’ll see where I consistently make incorrect decisions. Then, I can correct them!

Realise my weaknesses and run from their effects. Don’t run from my weaknesses and realise their effects.

DEMAND feedback on how to grow. More feedback now, less redoing later.

Calmness is in what’s inside, not what’s outside.

Ex: I can hold my tongue in an argument, but still feel annoyed at the other. That isn’t being ‘calm’. I have more work to do.

Curiosity dissipates conflict. "I am mad" --> "Why am I mad?"

Authenticity is proportional to spontaneity.

Authenticity needs to be maintained, not just initiated.

Thank people I’ve known for a while for times I remember them vulnerable/authentic. Helps maintain that ‘standard’ in our relationship.
